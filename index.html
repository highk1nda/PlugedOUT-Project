Activity 1: Problem Definition

In today’s gaming community, many players struggle with social interaction outside of online spaces. Gamers often communicate through voice chat, matchmaking lobbies, or guilds, but these interactions rarely develop into meaningful real-life connections. As a result, individuals may feel isolated despite being part of large online communities. Our team identified this as the central problem — gamers do not socialize effectively, and many lack opportunities to meet new people in person. To solve this issue, we created PluggedOUT, a platform designed to connect gamers through real-world activities and events. Any user can host or join activities, allowing people with similar interests to meet, interact, and build friendships beyond the screen. These events can vary widely — from casual hangouts to competitive matches. For example, a user could host a bowling night for Nintendo enthusiasts, where participants compete for fun, socialize, and get to know each other. Others might host a Mario Bros gaming session, a board game evening, or even a watch party for an upcoming eSports tournament. The goal is simple: bring people together through shared experiences. In the demonstration video, we showcased this idea by illustrating how individuals who previously struggled to socialize were able to connect once they discovered PluggedOUT. One user hosted a Mario Bros game session, and through the platform, people who were once disconnected and shy came together, interacted naturally, and formed new friendships.
